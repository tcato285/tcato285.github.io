November 10, 1775

The Continental Marines

The Marine Corps was founded on Nov. 10, 1775 with the aim to have soldiers that are specialized in naval combat and assisting in land forces. 
Two Battalions of Marines were formed under the command of Captain Samuel Nicholas, who instructed Robert Mullan to train them. According to the standing legend, the first training center was at Tun Tavern in Philidelphia.

July 11, 1798

The Marine Corps

After the Continental Marines had provided support in the early military, it was a new nation by this time, Congress decided to establish the Navy and the Marine Corps as official branches. President John Adams signed the bill that established the Marine Corps on July 11, 1798.

They were formed during the French Revolution to protect the American shipping because the French Navy was harassing the American ships.

This group fought in numerous battles because of their ability to fight at sea and land. However, their first land battle was not until the War of 1812.

1798-2022

The Modern Marine Corps

Since 1798, the Marines have fought in almost every battle that the US has been involved in. They have undergone many changes to make them the elite force they are today. Today, they specialized and participate in airial, land, and naval combat. 

They have now adopted a flag with a red background and an eagle, anchor, and globe symbol representing their specialty in all types of combat.

Their motto is Semper Fidelis, meaning "Always Faithful."

Facts About the Marines

One may often hear a Marine called a "Devil Dog." That name has been carried with pride since the battle of Belleau Wood during WWI. The Germans were surprised at the ferocity in which they fought so they gave them that nickname. That is the one that has stuck, though there were other nicknames given to them throughout other wars like the term "leatherneck" due to their use of leather collars to protect the neck from Japanese troops with swords.

The Marines are actually a Department of the Navy, though they are a separate branch of the military.

Requirements for Marines

All Marines go through intense training called boot camp. This training includes intense physical exercise, land navigation, marching drills, leadership training, marksmanship, and combat skills. It is designed to wear the men down and build them better along with sifting out any that are not fit for the Marines. They have a high standard that can seem like perfection.

After the Marines graduate from boot camp, they are assigned a specialized job, where they have more specialized training.
